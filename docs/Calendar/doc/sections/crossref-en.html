calendar specificationc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/icity/Calendar/CalendarSpecification

A calendar specification refers to a date(s), which may be specified at varying levels of granularity. It is important to recognize that a date represents some recurring points/intervals in time and therefore a CalendarSpecification is distinct from a DateTimeDescription (in the OWL time ontology), which describes a specific point in time. For more on the distinction between dates and timepoints, we refer the interested reader to (Gr√ºninger, Michael. "Ontologies for Dates and Duration." KR. 2010).

day of weekc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://schema.org/DayOfWeek

A day of the week is a member of an enumerated class, covering the days of the week in the Gregorian calendar. Schema:DayOfWeek also includes a PublicHoliday individual that may be used to capture the distinction between a typical day of the week and a public holiday (e.g. where businesses may be closed or parking policies may vary).
is equivalent to
{ friday , monday , public holiday , saturday , sunday , thursday , tuesday , wednesday }

opening hours specificationc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/icity/Calendar/OpeningHoursSpecification

Opening hours specification is a specialization of a calendar specification that includes a representation of opening and closing times. It is useful in specifying the hours of operation such as for businesses, services, or the hours of effect for parking policies.
